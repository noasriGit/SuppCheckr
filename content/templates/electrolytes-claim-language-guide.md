# Electrolytes Claim Language Guide

Editorial reference for Electrolytes guides, ingredient pages, category hub, product reviews, and comparisons.

**Phase:** 14A (planning) — apply during future Electrolytes content drafts and all later phases.  
**Positioning:** SuppCheckr is an evidence-aware supplement comparison site, not a medical authority.

See also: [`electrolytes-product-research-checklist.md`](./electrolytes-product-research-checklist.md), [`electrolytes-source-inventory-2026-07-07.md`](./electrolytes-source-inventory-2026-07-07.md), NIH ODS sodium/potassium/magnesium fact sheets (cite in YAML — do not treat this doc as a medical source).

---

## Core principles

1. **Label-first:** Product copy describes what Supplement Facts, Nutrition Facts, and official brand materials show.
2. **Separate fact types:** Label facts, brand claims, verified testing, retail prices, and general safety background must not be conflated.
3. **No personal medical outcomes:** Scores and copy reflect label transparency and sourcing — not whether a product will hydrate, prevent cramps, or treat illness.
4. **Conservative by default:** When in doubt, soften language and add a consult-a-professional caveat.
5. **Human supplements only:** No animal/pet electrolyte products.
6. **Not medical rehydration:** Electrolyte drink mixes are compared as labeled products — not ORS, IV fluids, or clinical dehydration treatment.

## Allowed / conservative language

| Use case | Preferred phrasing |
|----------|-------------------|
| Comparison framing | "helps compare sodium, potassium, magnesium, sugar, sweeteners, and price per serving" |
| Active lifestyle context | "may be useful for people comparing electrolyte drink mixes for active lifestyles" |
| Label variability | "labels vary widely by sodium level, sugar content, sweeteners, and serving format" |
| Use-case diversity | "not all electrolyte products are designed for the same use case" |
| Nutrient context | "sodium and potassium are essential minerals involved in normal body processes" — with NIH citation |
| Label grounding | "based on the product label," "the Nutrition Facts panel lists," "according to publicly available information" |
| Individual variation | "individual needs vary," "tolerance and dietary context differ" |
| Professional referral | "talk with a qualified healthcare professional if pregnant, nursing, under 18, taking medication, managing blood pressure, kidney disease, heart disease, fluid restrictions, or a medical condition" |
| Price/value | "dated manual price," "price per serving," "check current price at the retailer" |
| Testing | "verified third-party testing (NSF listing cited)" vs "brand states testing without public certificate" |
| Scoring | "SuppCheckr score reflects label and sourcing quality — not personal medical suitability" |
| Sugar comparison | "one product contains X g sugar per serving; another is labeled sugar-free" |
| Format comparison | "stick packs vs tub powder vs ready-to-drink — serving practicality differs" |

## Avoid / disallowed language

Do **not** use these patterns in SuppCheckr Electrolytes content:

| Category | Examples to avoid |
|----------|-------------------|
| Dehydration treatment | "cures dehydration," "treats dehydration," "clinical rehydration," "replaces IV fluids" |
| Electrolyte imbalance | "treats electrolyte imbalance," "corrects low sodium," "fixes potassium deficiency" |
| Illness / fluid loss | "treats vomiting," "treats diarrhea," "replaces fluids lost from illness," "recovery from stomach bug" |
| Heat illness | "treats heat stroke," "treats heat exhaustion," "prevents heat illness" |
| Medical conditions | "treats POTS," "for kidney disease," "for heart failure," "for dialysis patients" |
| Hangover claims | "prevents hangovers," "cures hangovers," "morning-after recovery" |
| Performance guarantees | "guarantees performance," "boosts endurance," "maximizes hydration," "prevents bonking" |
| Cramp claims | "prevents cramps," "stops muscle cramps," "cramp cure" |
| Universal safety | "safe for everyone," "no side effects," "doctor recommended" (unless specifically sourced) |
| Demographic winners | "best for kids," "best for pregnancy," "best for athletes," "best for keto" as medical claims |
| Medical necessity | "medically necessary," "prescription-strength hydration" |
| Regulatory misstatements | "FDA-approved supplement," "FDA certified," "clinically proven" (unqualified) |
| Ranking overreach | "Best Overall," "Best for Everyone," "#1 hydration," "top medical choice" |
| Keto/fasting medical | "breaks a fast safely," "medically required for keto," "treats keto flu" |
| Animal/pet | dog, cat, horse, veterinary electrolyte dosing or products |

## Caution topics

Handle these with extra care — FAQ or safety sections only, not first-wave landing pages:

| Topic | Guidance |
|-------|----------|
| **High sodium products** | Cite NIH sodium context; note blood pressure and kidney disease considerations; no universal safety |
| **Sugar-containing products** | Label facts only; no "healthier hydration" claims vs sugar-free |
| **Kidney disease** | General safety referral; no dosing advice; high potassium/sodium products especially sensitive |
| **Heart disease / CHF** | Fluid and sodium restrictions — clinician context only |
| **High blood pressure** | Sodium content is a label fact; no product suitability claims |
| **Diuretics / BP medications** | Interaction context with citation; refer to pharmacist/clinician |
| **Pregnancy / nursing** | No product picks; general referral to healthcare professional |
| **Children** | No pediatric product rankings; refer parents to pediatric clinicians |
| **Endurance sports** | Sweat/sodium context only; no performance outcome promises |
| **Fasting / keto marketing** | Mirror brand positioning as `brand_claim`; do not endorse medical fasting protocols |
| **Hangover marketing** | Reject in SuppCheckr copy even if competitors use it |
| **Illness / dehydration search intent** | Redirect to label comparison framing; do not answer as medical treatment |
| **POTS / dysautonomia** | High claim-risk search adjacency — label facts only; no condition suitability |
| **ORS / Pedialyte-style products** | Compare labels if included; no illness-treatment framing |

## Standard safety block (use on guides, category hub, product reviews)

Include a variant of this on every Electrolytes page:

> Dietary supplements and electrolyte drink mixes are not FDA-approved to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease. They are not a substitute for medical care, oral rehydration therapy, or treatment of dehydration or electrolyte imbalance. This page summarizes publicly available label and reference information — not medical advice. Consult a qualified healthcare professional before starting or changing supplements or high-sodium electrolyte products, especially if pregnant, nursing, under 18, taking medication, managing blood pressure, kidney disease, heart disease, fluid restrictions, or a medical condition.

## Format-specific cautions

### Stick packs / powder tubs

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| State sodium, potassium, magnesium mg per serving from label | "Optimal hydration formula" |
| Note sugar g or "sugar-free" from label | "Replenishes exactly what you sweat out" |
| Compare price per serving | "Prevents workout failure" |

### Ready-to-drink (RTD)

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Report sodium and sugar per bottle and per serving if label differs | "Better than water for dehydration" |
| Note artificial colors/flavors when disclosed | "Medical-grade hydration" |

### Tablets

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Document tablets per serving and dissolve instructions | "Instant cramp relief" |

### Sugar-free vs sugar-containing

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Compare carbohydrate and calorie disclosure | "Sugar is always worse" or "sugar is always better" |
| Name sweeteners from label | Hide dextrose in "hydration blend" marketing |

## LMNT vs Liquid IV specific cautions

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Compare sodium mg, sugar g, sweeteners, price per serving from labels | "LMNT is medically better" or "Liquid IV cures dehydration" |
| Note LMNT is typically higher sodium, lower/no sugar | "One is for athletes, one is for everyone" |
| Note Liquid IV typically contains sugar and different sodium level | Hangover or illness recovery framing |
| Frame as "label comparison for shoppers" | Workout performance guarantees |

## Claim type → language mapping (YAML editorial)

When drafting claims in product/guide YAML:

| claimType | Safe content examples |
|-----------|----------------------|
| `label_fact` | "Nutrition Facts list 1,000 mg sodium and 0 g sugar per stick serving." |
| `price_fact` | "Manual price observed $X on [date]; price per serving ≈ $Y." |
| `brand_claim` | "The brand product page states [claim] — not independently verified by SuppCheckr." |
| `health_context` | "According to NIH ODS materials, sodium is involved in fluid balance — cite required." |
| `safety_context` | "People with kidney disease or fluid restrictions should consult a clinician before high-sodium products — cite required." |
| `editorial_opinion` | "Among reviewed products, this label clearly states sodium and potassium mg per serving — editorial assessment." |

Never assign `health_context` or `safety_context` without authoritative citations (NIH, FDA, consensus_body).

## Quick review checklist (before draft sign-off)

- [ ] No dehydration/illness treatment verbs (treat, cure, rehydrate medically, prevent hangover)
- [ ] No "Best Overall" / "Best for Everyone"
- [ ] Sodium/potassium/magnesium mg per serving referenced where electrolyte content is discussed
- [ ] Sugar and sweetener disclosure captured where relevant
- [ ] Brand marketing claims labeled as brand claims, not facts
- [ ] Testing status matches evidence tier (`verified` vs `claimed`)
- [ ] Consult-professional block present
- [ ] No cramp prevention or performance guarantee endorsements
- [ ] No Amazon reviews cited as evidence
- [ ] No animal/pet supplement framing
