# CoQ10 Claim Language Guide

Editorial reference for CoQ10 guides, ingredient pages, category hub, product reviews, and comparisons.

**Phase:** 15A (planning) — apply during future CoQ10 content drafts and all later phases.  
**Positioning:** SuppCheckr is an evidence-aware supplement comparison site, not a medical authority.

See also: [`coq10-product-research-checklist.md`](./coq10-product-research-checklist.md), [`coq10-source-inventory-2026-07-07.md`](./coq10-source-inventory-2026-07-07.md), NIH ODS CoQ10 fact sheet and NCCIH overview (cite in YAML — do not treat this doc as a medical source).

---

## Core principles

1. **Label-first:** Product copy describes what Supplement Facts and official brand materials show.
2. **Separate fact types:** Label facts, brand claims, verified testing, retail prices, and general safety background must not be conflated.
3. **No personal medical outcomes:** Scores and copy reflect label transparency and sourcing — not whether a product will improve heart health, energy, fertility, or statin-related symptoms.
4. **Conservative by default:** When in doubt, soften language and add a consult-a-professional caveat.
5. **Human supplements only:** No animal/pet CoQ10 products.
6. **Not cardiovascular treatment:** CoQ10 supplements are compared as labeled products — not prescriptions, statin substitutes, or heart-disease therapy.

## Allowed / conservative language

| Use case | Preferred phrasing |
|----------|-------------------|
| Comparison framing | "helps compare CoQ10 form, dose, serving size, added ingredients, testing, and price per 100 mg" |
| Form variability | "CoQ10 products vary by ubiquinone/ubiquinol form, capsule/softgel format, carrier oils, and testing transparency" |
| Label literacy | "label clarity matters because CoQ10 products often differ in dose and form" |
| Non-medical scope | "this comparison is not medical advice and does not rank products for heart disease or medication-related use" |
| Nutrient context | "CoQ10 is a compound involved in normal cellular energy processes" — with NIH/NCCIH citation |
| Label grounding | "based on the product label," "the Supplement Facts panel lists," "according to publicly available information" |
| Form comparison | "one product lists ubiquinol; another lists ubiquinone — compare label facts, not assumed medical superiority" |
| Price/value | "dated manual price," "price per 100 mg CoQ10," "check current price at the retailer" |
| Testing | "verified third-party testing (USP/NSF listing cited)" vs "brand states testing without public certificate" |
| Scoring | "SuppCheckr score reflects label and sourcing quality — not personal medical suitability" |
| Professional referral | "talk with a qualified healthcare professional if pregnant, nursing, under 18, taking medications, taking blood thinners, preparing for surgery, managing a heart condition, liver/kidney disease, or a medical condition" |

## Avoid / disallowed language

Do **not** use these patterns in SuppCheckr CoQ10 content:

| Category | Examples to avoid |
|----------|-------------------|
| Heart disease | "treats heart disease," "prevents heart disease," "treats heart failure," "best for heart health" |
| Cardiovascular outcomes | "lowers blood pressure," "lowers cholesterol," "improves cardiac function" |
| Statin adjacency | "replaces statins," "statin side-effect treatment," "take instead of statins," "best for statin users" |
| Medication advice | "stop your medication," "adjust your statin dose," "warfarin patients should take" |
| Fertility | "fertility treatment," "improves egg quality," "best for conception" |
| Neurological | "Parkinson's treatment," "migraine cure," "prevents migraines" |
| Anti-aging / energy | "anti-aging cure," "reverses aging," "boosts energy guaranteed," "cellular energy miracle" |
| Universal safety | "safe for everyone," "no side effects," "doctor recommended" (unless specifically sourced per product) |
| Regulatory misstatements | "FDA-approved supplement," "FDA certified," "clinically proven" (unqualified) |
| Demographic winners | "best for seniors," "best for athletes," "best for women," "best for men" as medical claims |
| Form superiority | "ubiquinol is always better," "ubiquinone is useless," "3x absorption guaranteed" (unless narrow product-specific sourced brand claim mirrored conservatively) |
| Ranking overreach | "Best Overall," "Best for Everyone," "#1 heart supplement," "top cardiologist pick" |
| Animal/pet | dog, cat, veterinary CoQ10 dosing or products |

## Caution topics

Handle these with extra care — FAQ or safety sections only, not first-wave landing pages:

| Topic | Guidance |
|-------|----------|
| **Blood thinners / warfarin** | NIH/NCCIH interaction context; refer to clinician/pharmacist; no dosing advice |
| **Heart medications** | General interaction caution; no product suitability for CHF, AFib, etc. |
| **Statin-adjacent search intent** | Redirect to label comparison; do not advise starting/stopping statins or CoQ10 for statin side effects |
| **Pregnancy / nursing** | No product picks; general referral to healthcare professional |
| **Surgery** | Discontinue/adjust supplement timing — clinician context only |
| **Chronic medical conditions** | Label facts only; no condition-specific rankings |
| **Heart disease / blood pressure** | High claim-risk search adjacency — conservative framing only |
| **Fertility / migraine / Parkinson's search intent** | Reject as primary page framing; brief FAQ redirect to professional care if needed |
| **High-dose products (200 mg+)** | Label facts and price context; no medical dosing advice |
| **Combo products (PQQ, omega-3)** | Score formula complexity; defer in wave 1 if possible |
| **Soy / gelatin / allergen concerns** | Capture from label; no universal safety claims |
| **Ubiquinol premium pricing** | Price-per-100-mg comparison; no absorption superiority without sourced caution |

## Standard safety block (use on guides, category hub, product reviews)

Include a variant of this on every CoQ10 page:

> Dietary supplements are not FDA-approved to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease. CoQ10 supplements are not a substitute for medical care or prescribed cardiovascular treatment. This page summarizes publicly available label and reference information — not medical advice. Do not start, stop, or change medications based on supplement information on this site. Consult a qualified healthcare professional before starting or changing CoQ10 supplements, especially if pregnant, nursing, under 18, taking medications (including blood thinners), preparing for surgery, managing a heart condition, liver or kidney disease, or another medical condition.

## Form-specific cautions

### Ubiquinone softgels

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| State mg CoQ10 per serving from label | "Converts to ubiquinol in your body — guaranteed benefit" |
| Note carrier oil when disclosed | "Better for heart patients" |

### Ubiquinol softgels

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| State ubiquinol mg per serving from label | "Always absorbs 3x better than ubiquinone" (unless narrow sourced brand claim) |
| Compare price per 100 mg across forms | "Medically superior form" |

### Combo stacks (PQQ, BioPerine, omega-3)

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| List added actives from label | "Synergistic heart stack" |
| Note scoring complexity in editorial | "Replaces multiple medications" |
